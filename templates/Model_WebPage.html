Lepilens

What is Lepilens?


Lepilens is an iOS and Web application for automatic classification of butterfly (Lepidoptera) photos employing deep learning techniques. Currently, Lepilens can classify 134 different species of diurnal butterflies that can be found in Portugal and across Europe.


Lepilens aims to connect its users with nature and butterflies, and help citizen science efforts in mapping biodiversity. Butterflies play an essential role in the natural ecosystem as pollinators and as food for other animals in all stages of their life cycle. Their intricate relationships with plants and animals means that they are often the first to be endangered if an ecosystem’s delicate balance is disturbed. Thus, the occurrence of certain species and their numbers are often used as indicators of ecosystem health.




Try it out!


Provide photos of butterflies in JPEG or PNG format with at least 300x300 pixels (high resolution is not required). Better results are obtained for close-up photos of single butterflies (crop photos if required). Inception

Lepilens was developed by Tomás M. S. Mamede in the scope of a project of the Computer Science BSc degree at DCC/FCUP, under the supervision of Luís M. B. Lopes and Eduardo R. B. Marques.


For further details check:
  On using Deep Learning for Automatic Taxonomic Identification of Butterflies (Tomás M. S. Mamede project report)
  the GitHub repositories for the iOS and Web versions of Lepilens.

The Lepilens deep learning model was built using cloud services generously provided through the Google Cloud research credits program. The model was trained using selected photos mostly taken from the iNaturalist Research-grade Observations dataset, published at GBIF by iNaturalist. Complementary training photos for less common species were taken from:

  • Lepidoptera Portugal Facebook group
  • Lusoborboletas by Pedro Pires
  • Moths and Butterflies of Europe and North Africa by Paolo Mazzei, Daniel Morel, Raniero Panfili
  • Butterflies in Hungary by Ádám Gór
  • Lepidoptera and their ecology by Wolfgang Wagner
  • Lepiforum by Erwin Rennwald and Jürgen Rodeland
  • euroButterflies by Matt Rowlings
  • Butterflies of France by Roger Gibbons
  • UK Butterflies by Peter Eeles
